Math.AP: Analysis of PDEs

Flows of vector fields: classical and modern

April 13, 2020 3:00pm UTC
April 13, 2020 11:00am UTC -04 (Host local time)
Speaker: Camillo De Lellis (Institute for Advanced Study) - Hosted by Institute for Advanced Study

Abstract:

Consider a (possibly time-dependent) vector field $v$ on the Euclidean space. The classical Cauchy-Lipschitz (also named Picard-Lindel"of) Theorem states that, if the vector field $v$ is Lipschitz in space, for every initial datum $x$ there is a unique trajectory $\gamma$ starting at $x$ at time $0$ and solving the ODE $\dot{\gamma} (t) = v (t, \gamma (t))$.
