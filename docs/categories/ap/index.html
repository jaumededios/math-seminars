Math.AP: Analysis of PDEs

Flows of vector fields: classical and modern

April 13, 2020 3:00pm UTC
April 13, 2020 11:00am UTC -04 (Host local time)
Speaker: Camillo De Lellis (Institute for Advanced Study) - Hosted by Institute for Advanced Study

Abstract:
Consider a (possibly time-dependent) vector field $v$ on the Euclidean space. The classical Cauchy-Lipschitz (also named Picard-Lindel"of) Theorem states that, if the vector field $v$ is Lipschitz in space, for every initial datum $x$ there is a unique trajectory $\gamma$ starting at $x$ at time $0$ and solving the ODE $\dot{\gamma} (t) = v (t, \gamma (t))$. Geometric stochastic PDEs

April 28, 2020 4:00pm UTC
April 28, 2020 9:00am UTC -07 (Host local time)
Speaker: Martin Hairer (Imperial College London) - Hosted by UCLA

Abstract:
We review Parisi and Wu’s stochastic quantisation procedure and apply it to the non-linear sigma model as well as the Yang-Mills model. We then review a number of recent results on the resulting equations. In particular, this sheds some new light on an old controversy regarding the interpretation of path integrals. (...)

Introduction to regularity structures 1

April 29, 2020 4:00pm UTC
April 29, 2020 9:00am UTC -07 (Host local time)
Speaker: Martin Hairer (Imperial College London) - Hosted by UCLA

Abstract:
In these two lectures, I will give an introduction to the main concepts behind the theory of regularity structures which allows to give meaning to many stochastic PDEs that were previously thought to be ill-posed. This combines some of the insights and tools from (perturbative) QFT with classical PDE theory. (...)
