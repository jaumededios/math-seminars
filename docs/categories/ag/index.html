Math.AG: Algebraic Geometry

Graph rigidity and measurement varieties

April 9, 2020 3:40pm UTC
April 9, 2020 5:40pm UTC +02 (Host local time)
Speaker: Louis Theran (University of St. Andrews) - Hosted by NASO

Abstract:

Geometric rigidity theory is concerned with how much information about a configuration p of n points in a d-dimensional Euclidean space is determined by pairwise Euclidean distance measurements, indexed by the edges of a graph G with n vertices. One can turn this around, and, define, for a fixed graph G, a “measurement variety” associated with all possible edge lengths measurements as the configuration varies. (...)

The punctual Hilbert scheme of 4 points in affine 3-space via computer algebra and representation theory

April 14, 2020 4:20pm UTC
April 14, 2020 6:20pm UTC +02 (Host local time)
Speaker: Balas Szendroi (University of Oxford) - Hosted by NASO

Abstract:

The n-th punctual Hilbert scheme $Hilb_0^n(A^d)$ of points of affine d-space parametrises ideals of finite co-length n of the ring of functions on d-dimensional affine space, whose radical is the maximal ideal at the origin (equivalently, subschemes of length n with support at the origin). A classical theorem of Briancon claims the irreducibility of this space for d=2 and arbitrary n. (...)
