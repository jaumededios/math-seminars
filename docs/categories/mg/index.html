Math.MG: Metric Geometry

Graph rigidity and measurement varieties

April 9, 2020 3:40pm UTC
April 9, 2020 5:40pm UTC +02 (Host local time)
Speaker: Louis Theran (University of St. Andrews) - Hosted by NASO

Abstract:

Geometric rigidity theory is concerned with how much information about a configuration p of n points in a d-dimensional Euclidean space is determined by pairwise Euclidean distance measurements, indexed by the edges of a graph G with n vertices. One can turn this around, and, define, for a fixed graph G, a “measurement variety” associated with all possible edge lengths measurements as the configuration varies. (...)

April 16, 2020 6:00pm UTC
April 16, 2020 2:00pm UTC -04 (Host local time)
Speaker: Merlin Incerti-Medici (University of Zurich) - Hosted by Ohio State University

Abstract:

Given a CAT(-1) space, we can associate to it a boundary at infinity and a cross ratio on said boundary. There is a series of results that tell us that, for sufficiently nice CAT(-1) spaces, the boundary together with the cross ratio uniquely determines the interior space. (...)
