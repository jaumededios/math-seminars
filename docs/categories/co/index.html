Recent Progress on the Erdos-Hajnal Conjecture

April 7, 2020 6:00pm UTC
April 7, 2020 8:00pm CEST (Barcelona, Paris)
April 7, 2020 2:00pm EDT (New York, Boston)
April 7, 2020 11:00am PDT (Los Angeles)
April 7, 2020 2:00pm UTC -04 (Local Time)
Speaker: Maria Chudnovsky (Princeton University) - Hosted by Northeast Combinatorics Network

Abstract:

What is the effect of excluding an induced subgraph on the global structure of a graph? While there do not seem to be general structural consequences, a conjecture of Erdos and Hajnal states that graphs with forbidden induced subgraphs behave very differently from general graphs; more precisely they contain much larger cliques or stable sets. Pure Pairs

April 9, 2020 5:30pm UTC
April 9, 2020 7:30pm CEST (Barcelona, Paris)
April 9, 2020 1:30pm EDT (New York, Boston)
April 9, 2020 10:30am PDT (Los Angeles)
April 9, 2020 10:30am UTC -07 (Local Time)
Speaker: Paul Seymour (Princeton) - Hosted by Stanford University

Abstract:

A “pure pair” in a graph $G$ is a pair $(X,Y)$ of disjoint subsets of $V(G)$ such that either every vertex in $X$ is adjacent to every vertex in $Y$, or there are no edges from $X$ to $Y$. Let $f(G)$ be the biggest $k$ such that $G$ has a pure pair $(X,Y)$ with $|X|,|Y|$ at least $k$. (...)
