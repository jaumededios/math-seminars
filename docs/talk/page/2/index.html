Almost all Collatz orbits attain almost bounded values

April 8, 2020 12:00am UTC
April 7, 2020 5:00pm UTC -07 (Host local time)
Speaker: Terence Tao (UCLA) - Hosted by Caltech

Abstract:

Define the Collatz map Col on the natural numbers by setting Col(n) to equal 3n+1 when n is odd and n/2 when n is even. The notorious Collatz conjecture asserts that all orbits of this map eventually attain the value 1. This remains open, even if one is willing to work with almost all orbits rather than all orbits. Recent Progress on the Erdos-Hajnal Conjecture

April 7, 2020 6:00pm UTC
April 7, 2020 2:00pm UTC -04 (Host local time)
Speaker: Maria Chudnovsky (Princeton University) - Hosted by Northeast Combinatorics Network

Abstract:

What is the effect of excluding an induced subgraph on the global structure of a graph? While there do not seem to be general structural consequences, a conjecture of Erdos and Hajnal states that graphs with forbidden induced subgraphs behave very differently from general graphs; more precisely they contain much larger cliques or stable sets.
