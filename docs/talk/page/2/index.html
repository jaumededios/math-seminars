Total curvature and the isoperimetric inequality

April 7, 2020 11:00pm UTC
April 7, 2020 4:00pm UTC -07 (Host local time)
Speaker: Mohammad Ghomi (Georgia Tech) - Hosted by Caltech

Abstract:

The classical isoperimetric inequality states that in Euclidean space spheres provide enclosures of least perimeter for any given volume. According to the Cartan-Hadamard conjecture, this inequality may be generalized to spaces of nonpositive curvature. In this talk we discuss an approach to proving this conjecture via a comparison formula for the total curvature of level sets of functions on nonpositively curved manifolds. (...)

Recent Progress on the Erdos-Hajnal Conjecture

April 7, 2020 6:00pm UTC
April 7, 2020 2:00pm UTC -04 (Host local time)
Speaker: Maria Chudnovsky (Princeton University) - Hosted by Northeast Combinatorics Network

Abstract:

What is the effect of excluding an induced subgraph on the global structure of a graph? While there do not seem to be general structural consequences, a conjecture of Erdos and Hajnal states that graphs with forbidden induced subgraphs behave very differently from general graphs; more precisely they contain much larger cliques or stable sets.
