Recent Progress on the Erdos-Hajnal Conjecture

April 7, 2020 6:00pm UTC
April 7, 2020 8:00pm CEST (Barcelona, Paris)
April 7, 2020 2:00pm EDT (New York, Boston)
April 7, 2020 11:00am PDT (Los Angeles)
April 7, 2020 2:00pm UTC -04 (Local Time)
Speaker: Maria Chudnovsky (Princeton University) - Hosted by Northeast Combinatorics Network

Abstract:

What is the effect of excluding an induced subgraph on the global structure of a graph? While there do not seem to be general structural consequences, a conjecture of Erdos and Hajnal states that graphs with forbidden induced subgraphs behave very differently from general graphs; more precisely they contain much larger cliques or stable sets. A p-adic monodromy theorem for de Rham local systems

April 6, 2020 11:00pm UTC
April 7, 2020 1:00am CEST (Barcelona, Paris)
April 6, 2020 7:00pm EDT (New York, Boston)
April 6, 2020 4:00pm PDT (Los Angeles)
April 6, 2020 4:00pm UTC -07 (Local Time)
Speaker: Koji Shimizu (IAS) - Hosted by UCLA

Abstract:

Every smooth proper algebraic variety over a p-adic field is expected to have semistable model after passing to a finite extension. This conjecture is open in general, but its analogue for p-adic Galois representations, the p-adic monodromy theorem, is known. In this talk, we will explain a generalization of this theorem to etale local systems on a smooth rigid analytic variety. (...)
