Geometric stochastic PDEs

April 28, 2020 4:00pm UTC
April 28, 2020 9:00am UTC -07 (Host local time)
Speaker: Martin Hairer (Imperial College London) - Hosted by UCLA

Abstract:
We review Parisi and Wu’s stochastic quantisation procedure and apply it to the non-linear sigma model as well as the Yang-Mills model. We then review a number of recent results on the resulting equations. In particular, this sheds some new light on an old controversy regarding the interpretation of path integrals. (...)

A localic approach to the semantics of dependency, conflict, and concurrency

April 29, 2020 5:00pm UTC
April 29, 2020 10:00am UTC -07 (Host local time)
Speaker: Gershom Bazerman (Awake Security) - Hosted by U. C. Riverside

Abstract:
Petri nets have been of interest to applied category theory for some time. Back in the 1980s, one approach to their semantics was given by algebraic gadgets called “event structures.” We use classical techniques from order theory to study event structures without conflict restrictions (which we term “dependency structures with choice”) by their associated “traces”, which let us establish a one-to-one correspondence between DSCs and a certain class of locales. Derivation of the wave kinetic equation

April 30, 2020 3:00pm UTC
April 30, 2020 8:00am UTC -07 (Host local time)
Speaker: Yu Deng (University of Southern California) - Hosted by PDE Seminar via Zoom

Abstract:
The wave turbulence theory describes the nonequilibrium statistical mechanics for a large class of nonlinear dispersive systems. A major goal of this theory is to derive the wave kinetic equation, which predicts the behavior of macroscopic limits of ensemble averages for microscopic interacting systems. Usually this limit happens at a particular “kinetic time scale” in the “weak-nonlinearity” limit where the number of interacting modes goes to infinity while the nonlinearity strength goes to zero. The Hilbert scheme of infinite affine space

May 1, 2020 7:00pm UTC
May 1, 2020 12:00pm UTC -07 (Host local time)
Speaker: Burt Totaro (UCLA) - Hosted by Stanford University

Abstract:
I will discuss the Hilbert scheme of $d$ points in affine $n$-space, with some examples. This space has many irreducible components for $n$ at least 3 and is poorly understood. Nonetheless, in the limit where $n$ goes to infinity, we show that the Hilbert scheme of $d$ points in infinite affine space has a very simple homotopy type. (...)
