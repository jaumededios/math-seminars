Almost all Collatz orbits attain almost bounded values

April 8, 2020 12:00am UTC
April 7, 2020 5:00pm UTC -07 (Host local time)
Speaker: Terence Tao (UCLA) - Hosted by Caltech

Abstract:

Define the Collatz map Col on the natural numbers by setting Col(n) to equal 3n+1 when n is odd and n/2 when n is even. The notorious Collatz conjecture asserts that all orbits of this map eventually attain the value 1. This remains open, even if one is willing to work with almost all orbits rather than all orbits. On the Kudla-Rapoport conjecture

April 9, 2020 8:30pm UTC
April 9, 2020 4:30pm UTC -04 (Host local time)
Speaker: Chao Li (Columbia University) - Hosted by Princeton/IAS

Abstract:

The Kudla-Rapoport conjecture predicts a precise identity between the arithmetic intersection number of special cycles on unitary Rapoport-Zink spaces and the derivative of local representation densities of hermitian forms. It is a key local ingredient to establish the arithmetic Siegel-Weil formula and the arithmetic Rallis inner product formula, relating the height of special cycles on Shimura varieties to the derivative of Siegel Eisenstein series and L-functions.
